# **MY TEACHER IS RAGEBAITING ME**
## What do I do?
> reddit ahh story
---
### So recently my teacher has been ragebaiting me by turning the switch on and off on my table.

For context, our classroom has these weird tables with built-in power outlets and a little switch that controls them. I sit near the front because my eyesight is terrible and I usually keep my laptop plugged in for notes.

At first I thought it was an accident.

I would be typing, my laptop would stop charging, and I'd look down to see the switch was off. I'd turn it back on and continue with class.

Five minutes later: *click.*

Off again.

I looked around. Nobody was near me.

Turned it back on.

Another few minutes passed.

*click.*

Off.

This happened like six times in one lesson.

Eventually I looked up and saw my teacher standing next to my desk pretending to organize papers. The second he noticed me looking at him, he walked away.

I didn't think much of it.

Until it started happening every single day.

Every lesson.

Without fail.

I'd be taking notes and hear the faintest little *click* from beneath my desk. Sometimes he'd walk by and casually switch it off. Sometimes he'd do it while explaining something to the class.

One day I literally watched him maintain eye contact with me while slowly pressing the switch down.

No words.

No expression.

Just *click.*

Then he continued teaching as if nothing happened.

My friend sitting next to me started noticing it too.

"Dude."

"What?"

"He just did it again."

"I know."

"No, I mean he looked excited."

And honestly?

He wasn't wrong.

My teacher had this tiny smile every time it happened.

Like he was running a social experiment.

I started keeping score.

Monday: 11 switch-offs.

Tuesday: 14.

Wednesday: 9.

Thursday: 17.

At that point I was more invested in the statistics than the actual class.

Then things escalated.

I bought a small power bank so my laptop wouldn't die.

The next lesson I proudly plugged it in.

My teacher walked over.

Looked at the power bank.

Looked at me.

Nodded.

And walked away.

I thought I'd won.

Five minutes later he returned carrying a stack of worksheets.

He set them down directly on top of the power bank.

Not enough to damage it.

Just enough to disconnect the cable.

My friend nearly choked trying not to laugh.

The teacher glanced at us.

Smiled.

And continued teaching.

At this point I genuinely believed I was being targeted.

So I decided to confront him after class.

I waited until everyone left.

"Sir?"

"Yes?"

"Why do you keep turning my switch off?"

There was a long silence.

He looked at me.

Looked at the table.

Looked back at me.

Then he said:

"I was wondering how long it would take you to ask."

"What?"

"The switch doesn't control your outlet."

"...what?"

"It controls the lamp on my desk."

I looked over.

There was, in fact, a lamp.

A lamp I had never noticed before.

A lamp that had apparently been flickering on and off for the past three weeks.

My teacher started laughing.

Not loudly.

Just the laugh of a man who had successfully committed to the dumbest bit imaginable.

Apparently every time I turned the switch back on, he turned it back off because he thought *I* was messing with *him*.

For three entire weeks we had both been silently undoing each other's actions.

Neither of us said anything.

Neither of us questioned it.

We just accepted that the other person was our mortal enemy.

The next day he put a sticky note on the switch that said:

> **"TRUCE?"**

I turned it on.

He turned it off.

The war continues.
